Participants:

Jason (Head of Infrastructure, Prospect)
Priya (Security Engineer, Prospect)
Daniel (Sales Engineer, Vendor)

Jason:
We’ve been trying to clean up access for a while, but it’s kind of grown organically. Different teams do different things.

Priya:
Yeah, for production access we’re pretty strict, but for lower environments it’s all over the place. There’s not really a consistent policy.

Daniel:
How are you handling access today across those environments?

Jason:
It depends. Some teams use IAM roles, some use shared credentials, and then there’s still a VPN in the mix for certain systems.

Priya:
And honestly, visibility is a big issue. If someone asks who accessed what last week, it takes a while to piece that together.

Daniel:
Got it. And is that something that’s becoming more of a priority now?

Priya:
Yeah, definitely. We’ve got more scrutiny from security reviews. Auditability is becoming a requirement, not just a nice-to-have.

Jason:
Also, we’re scaling pretty quickly. What worked when we had 20 engineers doesn’t really work now.

Daniel:
That makes sense. One of the things we focus on is creating a consistent access model across environments with centralized visibility.

Jason:
So is this something that replaces our current setup, or sits alongside it?

Daniel:
It usually integrates with what you already have and standardizes access patterns.

Priya:
I think my concern is around enforcement. Like, how do you make sure people actually follow the new model?

Jason:
Yeah, and how opinionated is it? We’ve got some pretty specific workflows already.

Daniel:
It’s flexible, but it does introduce a more consistent framework.

Priya:
Okay, but that’s where I worry. Sometimes “framework” means we have to change a lot of how we work.

Jason:
And what does rollout look like? Is this something we can do incrementally?

Daniel:
Typically, yes—it can be phased in over time.

Priya:
I think for us, the big questions are:

how enforceable this actually is
how much change it requires
and how quickly we can see value

Jason:
Yeah, and whether it actually reduces overhead, or just shifts it somewhere else.
