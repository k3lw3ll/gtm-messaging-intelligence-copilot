Competitor Notes

Overview

Organizations evaluating infrastructure access solutions often compare multiple approaches rather than a single direct competitor.

These typically include identity providers, access management tools, and existing internal solutions.

Common Alternatives
Identity and Access Management (IAM) Platforms

Examples: AWS IAM, Azure AD, Okta

These systems manage user identity and authentication across applications and cloud services.

Strengths:

widely adopted
integrated into cloud platforms
familiar to engineering teams

Limitations:

not designed to manage infrastructure access across multiple environments
often rely on static roles and policies
limited visibility across heterogeneous systems
VPN-Based Access

Traditional approach using network-level access controls.

Strengths:

simple mental model
widely deployed

Limitations:

grants broad network access rather than scoped resource access
difficult to audit at a granular level
creates security risks through over-permissioning
Homegrown Access Solutions

Custom scripts, internal tools, or ad hoc processes built over time.

Strengths:

tailored to specific environments
flexible

Limitations:

inconsistent across teams
difficult to maintain and scale
lack of centralized visibility and control
Common Customer Perceptions
“We already have IAM, so this feels redundant”
“This seems like another layer on top of existing tools”
“Not clear how this replaces what we already use”
“Concern about adding complexity instead of reducing it”
Positioning Challenges
Differentiation from IAM is not always clearly understood
The shift from network-based access to identity-based access is conceptually new for some teams
Messaging often emphasizes capabilities but not day-to-day workflows
Lack of concrete examples makes it harder for technical buyers to evaluate impact
