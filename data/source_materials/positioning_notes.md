Positioning Notes

Core Positioning

The platform is positioned as an infrastructure access solution that replaces fragmented, network-based approaches with a unified, identity-driven model.

It is intended to serve as a central control plane for access across modern environments, including cloud, on-prem systems, and containerized workloads.

Key Differentiators
Identity-first access model rather than network-based access
Use of short-lived, ephemeral credentials instead of long-lived secrets
Centralized visibility across multiple environments
Consistent access patterns across heterogeneous systems
Conceptual Shift

The platform requires a shift from:

network location as the basis of trust
to
identity and context as the basis of trust

This change is often not immediately intuitive for teams accustomed to traditional VPN or IAM-based models.

Target Audience

Primary audience includes:

platform engineering teams
infrastructure and DevOps engineers
security teams responsible for access control

These users are typically technical and evaluate solutions based on:

architectural clarity
integration complexity
operational impact
security posture
Buying Considerations

Common evaluation criteria include:

how the platform integrates with existing identity systems
whether it replaces or complements existing tools
the level of disruption required to adopt
clarity of the operational model
auditability and compliance capabilities
Messaging Challenges
The distinction between identity-based access and existing IAM solutions is not always clearly understood
The platform can be perceived as an additional layer rather than a simplification
Messaging often focuses on capabilities rather than day-to-day workflows
Lack of concrete examples makes it difficult for buyers to map the solution to their environment
Desired Position

The platform should be understood as:

a unifying layer that simplifies access across environments
a replacement for fragmented and inconsistent access patterns
a system that reduces complexity rather than adding to it
Gaps to Watch
Overuse of abstract language without grounding in real workflows
Insufficient explanation of how the system changes daily operations
Limited emphasis on before-and-after comparisons
Unclear articulation of how this differs from existing identity tools
